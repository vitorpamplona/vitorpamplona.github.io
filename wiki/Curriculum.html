Curriculum

I am a scientist by heart, product developer by necessity, social entrepreneur by chance and community builder by philosophy. I am co-founder and CTO of EyeNetra and its service Blink, co-founder of PUB-Boston and co-founder and board member of the SciBr Foundation. My current goal is to create a suite of interconnected technological innovations that are able to find, measure and correct 2.5 billion people in the world that are today in need for simple eyeglasses and have no idea of their visual dysfunction. This is double the amount of users Facebook has today.  

I work on the triple frontier among optics, computer science and vision science. My work is mostly known for joining insights from computational photography and refractive science into new mass-market diagnostic devices for eye care (NETRA, Netrometer, Netropter, CATRA). I began by creating novel physiologically-based models of human eyes, algorithms for retinal imaging diagnostics and disruptions on eye relaxation and accommodation control procedures. To close the circle, I also elaborated the concept of vision correcting displays, a new way to correct for optical distortions beyond the usual eyewear (TailoredDisplays). These are all connected to EyeNetra's telemecine networks & data science platforms to provide comprehensive solutions to society. These award-winning ideas were patented and published in prestigious academic gatherings such as ACM SIGGRAPH, ARVO, AOA, and OSA Frontiers in Optics.

Most of my patents involve developing a low-cost patient-centric interactive component from the early science. The final products show significant improvements on user experience, allowing for mass-market consumer applications while keeping similar accuracies of clinical tools. Although research is in the essence of my work and the reason for why I am what I am, a significant part of my time is spent on working closely with product, quality, legal and market developers to deliver ground-shaking but sustainable entities, effectively immersing myself from the early science to the final impact.

In my second life, I work with PUBBoston and the SciBr Foundation to inspire, connect and mobilize scientific achievements and innovation development of Brazilians living abroad. I also co-produced the first year of the Science-based YouTube Channel Panorama Brasil Boston. I am an MIT short-term lecturer for executive MBA students and Eye Focus Accelerator mentor. I constantly review papers for ACM SIGGRAPH and others as well as grant applications for NSF and the XPrize Foundation. 

In Brazil, I am known for co-founding one of the largest collaboration platforms for software engineers, reaching 50k portuguese-speaking developers in the early 2000s (JavaFree), and the first portuguese-speaking forum for Ruby developers (RubyOnBr). During my early research days, I created the first 3D game engine for feature phones (M3GE) and the first image-based gesture-tracing glove (IBDG). I founded my first technology company when I was 15 years-old using my parent's " angel " investment, worked full-time on agile software development companies throughout my 5 years in college, and built 11 free software projects in the same time span. Other significant projects that I co-foudned were a credit card payments mobile app (PayDream) and an off-line-activity-engaging social network (Agitter).

I am a recipient of the Silver Thomas Edison Award 2012, NASA Health Innovation Award 2010, the Vodafone Innovation Award 2011, and my research was named as one of the biggest MIT achievements of 2010 by the Provost.

Co-Founded Start-ups

  1. Oct 2011 - Today: EyeNetra Inc. based on Cambridge, MA, USA.  
    Bringing affordable EyeTests to everyone using a mobile phone. Uncorrected refractive errors (i.e. nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism), presbyopia (age related eye condition) result in lost productivity, lost independence, illiteracy and impoverishment on a massive scale. Many in developing countries go without eye exams and remain unaware that they have a treatable eye condition. Leveraging the ubiquity and advanced hardware of mobile phones, EyeNETRA grew out of collaborative research at the MIT Media Lab and UFRGS Instituto de informática to develop and disseminate hardware apps – affordable and easy-to-use mobile phone attachments that provide recommendation for treatment.

Free Software Projects

  1. Fev 2011 - Hoje: FreeToDo : Syntax-organized To-Do list  
    Líder de Projeto, arquiteto e desenvolvedor. FreeToDo é um portal de acesso rápido a to-do lists pessoais. Feito em JSP e AJAX / Javascript, usa a Google Friend API para salvar um arquivo de to-do por usuário. O arquivo recebe real-time syntax highlighting (CodeMirror) durante a sua edição na web. É o primeiro projeto de to-do list onde o usuário é responsável pela estrutura de sua lista.

  2. Fev 2011 - Fev 2011: ShTweet: Command line twitter client.
    Um conjunto de scripts feitos em ShellScript para acesso ao Twitter sem usar a oAuth. O sistema usa CURL e Perl para simular um browser e realizar chamadas com usuários logados.
       
  3. Apr 2009 - Fev 2010: Weblessdoc : Unified offline documentation browser.
    Idealizador e desenvolvedor do projeto. Trata-se de um navegador de documentações off-line para linux. O aplicativo reúne, em uma interface simples, os diferentes estilos de documentação do " / usr / share / doc " a fim de facilitar a programação em ambientes off-line (durante viagens, por exemplo). O software é desenvolvido em Python e QT.

  4. May 2008 - Hoje: PLRModels : Pupil light reflex models.
    PLRModel is a library for several Pupil Light Reflex and Pupil Latency models for facial animations based on our work on Photorealistic Models for Pupil Light Reflex and Iridal Pattern Deformation. [Artigo, Tese, TechReport]

  5. Mai 2006 - Hoje: Priki : wiki wiki web com banco de dados OO.
    Líder de Projeto, arquiteto e desenvolvedor. Priki é uma engine de wiki cuja principal característica é o auto relacionamento entre páginas, criando uma rede de navegação. O Priki utiliza editores WYSIWYG para a edição de páginas, rotinas avançadas de segurança, e possui diversas configurações de acesso. Mantenho o servidor do projeto Wiki.com.br: administração do SVN, backups, e demais portais rodando no mesmo servidor. Software desenvolvido em Java com Webwork 2 e Prevayler.

  6. Fev 2005 - Feb 2007: Mobile 3D Game Engine (M3GE)
    Líder de projeto e arquiteto deste motor de jogos 3D para J2ME. O projeto é uma biblioteca que gerencia o rendering 3D, o fluxo do jogo, a carga e utilização de imagens, entrada e saída, detecção de colisão, multiplayer e outras facilidades. Atualmente, além da engine básica, a M3GE implementa multi-player através de blue-tooh e importa modelos do Quake II. Estes últimos recursos não foram implementados por mim.
